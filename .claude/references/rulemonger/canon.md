### [RTR 19950509] — Rulebook-provided actions are distinct when the chosen target differs; titles are not cards in play; {Madness Network} lets a torpid Malkavian act.
**Filed under:** {Change of Target}; {Madness Network}; {Obedience}; {Red Herring} (+1 more records)
**Source text:** original verbatim ([Google Groups](https://groups.google.com/g/rec.games.trading-cards.jyhad/c/_LKyR7pdMig/m/ZvwdGmIwUnsJ), fetched 2026-07-22)
**Status:** current

> Q: Obedience says that the acting minion may not repeat the neutralized action later in the turn. What exactly counts as "the same action"?
> A: Action cards may not be repeated this way, so that minion would not be able to Bum's Rush something else later that turn. The default actions provided by the game, and actions provided by cards in play, are considered distinct as long as you're choosing what gets affected. So a hunt or default bleed that was stopped by Obedience could not be attempted again later in the turn, but you could try to equip with a different piece of equipment, could try to burn a different Army of Rats, etc. All of this applies to Change Target as well.
>
> Titles are never considered to be cards in play, even though Praxis Seizures are often "played" on a vampire to remind everyone it has that title. This means that Democritus's ability does not apply to contested titles, Praxis: Solomon cannot dictate the outcome of a contested title, etc.
>
> A Malkavian in torpor may take advantage of Madness Network to rescue itself on another player's turn.

### [TOM 19951209] — A banished (uncontrolled) vampire keeps only blood and cards on it, and becomes controlled again once at or above capacity at influence-phase end.
**Filed under:** {Banishment}; {Lay Low}
**Source text:** original verbatim ([Google Groups](https://groups.google.com/g/rec.games.trading-cards.jyhad/c/qP2j6CpBUDI/m/2qsG4E23bl4J), fetched 2026-07-22)
**Status:** current for the return-to-play rule; the "loses all memory" stance was later reversed (RTR 20000501: banished vampires remember effects and titles).

> Q: So, if someone was foolish enough to Banish a full vampire they would come right back into play... neato!!
>
> A: Correct.
>
> Yes. If you have any vampires in your uncontrolled region that are at or above capacity at the end of your influence phase, the vampire(s) become controlled. How the vampires or the blood got there isn't important.
>
> Because a banished vampire becomes uncontrolled, it loses all memory; it only retains the blood it had, and any cards played on it. Any permanent changes like Ganger Deevolution will be erased, it will have its title back when it reappears, and so on. (At least, that's my answer; I'll find out if there's some arcane reason why this wouldn't be true.)

### [TOM 19960210] — Title-granted capacity is lost with the title; effects pointing at an uncontrolled vampire resume when it returns to play.
**Filed under:** group "Title providing capacity" (G00038); {Banishment}; {Lay Low}
**Source text:** original verbatim ([Google Groups](https://groups.google.com/g/rec.games.trading-cards.jyhad/c/PiOmH08RyVw/m/CqzTRcOLDNIJ), fetched 2026-07-22)
**Status:** current — the quoted items stand; the thread's companion claim that banished vampires forget applied effects was later reversed (RTR 20000501).

> Q: I have 2 Praxis Seizure: Paris […] and I had Delilah out (2 capacity). She had already been made Prince of Paris, and being Toreador, her capacity increased by one. I called the vote a second time later in the game, and I argued that her capacity did increase a second time […]
>
> A: The title itself is what gives her the 1 capacity. If you remove the title, she loses the 1 capacity (and excess blood), even if you happen to be replacing it with an identical title. After becoming prince of Paris a second time, Delilah was back to 3 capacity, not boosted to 4.
>
> Q: Also, let's say a vampire gains one of those titles that increase capacity and then lose their title due to Command of the Harpies or yeilding to a contested title. Do they lose the extra capacity? […]
>
> A: They gain the 1 capacity as long as they have that title. If the title goes, for any reason, so does the capacity.
>
> Q: A Banished vampire remembers only two things: how much blood he has and what cards are played on him. Other effects are ended. [RTR]
>
> A: The actual principle here is that "for the rest of the game" effects generated by the Banished vampire end, whereas such effects pointing at it will resume if/when the vampire comes back into play.
>
> Q: But effects generated by a vampire via a card in play do remain with the banished vampire, correct? […] Spying Mission, superior.
>
> A: Ah. Since the bleed-boost is provided by the SM card, and the Banished vampire retains this card, it will still be able to cash in the Spying Mission if it returns to play, yes.

### [LSJ 19970224] — Rules Team answers on Sabbat cards: granted abilities persist as scheduled, special hunts require a vampire target, reversed diablerie confers nothing.
**Filed under:** group "Steal blood as a Hunt action" (G00121); {Ashes to Ashes}; {Bloodbath}; {Camarilla Vitae Slave} (+6 more records)
**Source text:** original verbatim ([Google Groups](https://groups.google.com/g/rec.games.trading-cards.jyhad/c/80KRDjVFkyg/m/TBJso9Ra_9QJ), fetched 2026-07-22)
**Status:** current

> Q ({Bloodbath}): "If the target vampire later gains a title, will that new title cause him to lose the vote he gains from Bloodbath?"
> A: "Yes. Bloodbath gives an ability to the target. This ability is 'gain one extra vote unless you have a title' […]"
>
> Q ({Camarilla Vitae Slave}): "If the retainer is burned before your next master phase, does this text still apply 'from beyond the grave'?"
> A: "Yes. The vampire has been granted an ability which will last for a definite period of time […]"
>
> Q ({Dirty Little Secrets}): "If you successfully bleed a methuselah, but another Meth pays the cost (due to a Major Boon), whose library is affected?"
> A: "Effectively: No one's. Since the Methuselah you are bleeding […] loses zero pool, she burns zero cards."
>
> Q ({Machine Blitz}): "This means 'amount of damage that would be inflicted by the weapon'? Or 'amount the weapon actually inflicts this round/strike'?"
> A: "The former. The 'X' is set when the strike is announced, so won't change if the chosen weapon is destroyed."
>
> Q ({Reform Body}): "If a diabolist succeeds, but is faced with a Reformed Body, does the Diabolist still get the blood and/or equipment?"
> A: "No and no. The diablerie is considered unsuccessful […]"
>
> Q ({Ethan Locke}): "Is this action directed at the vampire, or just at the vampire's controller?"
> A: "At the skill card, and thus the Methuselah who controls the skill card."
>
> Q ({Legacy of Caine}): "What happens if there are no vampires with blood on them? Does he still (D) hunt on one of them? What if there are no other vampires at all?"
> A: "If he hunts, he must still take the (D) action vs. a vampire, even if there are no vampires with blood to steal. When hunting on a target that has no blood to steal, a successful action will simply have no effect (stealing zero blood). If there are no targets (vampires) at all, then he cannot hunt."

### [RTR 19970630] — Damage is dealt by a minion only via strikes or explicit card text; other damage is environmental and sourceless; only vampires commit diablerie.
**Filed under:** group "Allies who can play as a vampire" (G00011); group "Action continues not specifically from block" (G00098); group "Set range" (G00127); group "Adding damage to strikes" (G00148) (+40 more records)
**Source text:** original verbatim ([Google Groups](https://groups.google.com/g/rec.games.trading-cards.jyhad/c/KireUeOYY3c/m/t6ifm9lur1kJ), fetched 2026-07-22)
**Status:** current

> "If a minion plays a combat card that deals damage, the damage is considered dealt by that minion only if it results from a strike (including strike modifiers such as ammo cards) or if the effect specifically says the damage is dealt by the minion. Damage from other effects is considered "environmental," and has no particular source."
>
> "Only vampires may commit diablerie. Effects that would allow something other than a vampire to commit diablerie are ignored."
>
> "Form of Mist cannot "continue" an action if played during a combat resulting from a successful action, such as Bum's Rush."
>
> "Storm Sewers sets the range for the combat and prevents anyone from choosing that combat's range, as well as eliminating the Establish Range step of combat."
>
> "Effects that increase the damage provided by strike cards affect those cards rather than the striking minion. For example, if a vampire plays Increased Strength followed by Burning Wrath, Increased Strength's effect boosts the aggravated damage dealt by Burning Wrath […]"

### [LSJ 19980105] — Effects played "as the action would succeed" ({Spying Mission}) come at resolution; no further modifiers or reactions, e.g. {Archon Investigation}, may follow.
**Filed under:** group "Change the target of a bleed" (G00106); {Archon Investigation}; {Lost in Translation}; {Spying Mission}
**Source text:** original verbatim ([Google Groups](https://groups.google.com/g/rec.games.trading-cards.jyhad/c/PzVC-AeFuUQ/m/ncbtQyYNpFQJ), fetched 2026-07-22)
**Status:** current

> Q: If a vampire has superior Spying Mission played on him after a successful bleed of his prey, bleeds again
> successfully, then activates the +2 bleed. Can the prey then bleed bounce?
> A: No. Spying Mission is only burned after all action modifiers/reactions are played (when the action finally
> resolves), and then only if the proper Methuselah is being bled for one or more pool.
>
> Q: Similarly, if a vamp with Spying Mission played on him successfully bleeds for two, and then burns the Spying
> Mission for a total bleed of 4, can the Methuselah being bled now play Archon Investigation?
> A: Hmm. When you put it that way... Right. AI cannot be played after Spying Mission has been burned, since the
> bleed is resolved at that point.
>
> Q: After one has played superior Spying Mission on a bleeding vamp, is it too late to play Mask of 1000 Faces to
> untap him, and have another vamp take over the action?
> A: Yes, it is too late. Spying Mission is played "instead of removing pool", which means "when the action
> resolves" - further modifiers cannot be played, unless they are played after the action in general (like Freak
> Drive - but it cannot be played after an unblocked bleed of zero, so...)

### [RTR 19980623] — Changing a location's controller has no side effects absent card text; combat is the final step of a successful block, preceding after-block reactions.
**Filed under:** group "Changes controller of location" (G00066); {Anathema}; {Brujah Frenzy}; {Cats' Guidance} (+9 more records)
**Source text:** original verbatim ([VEKN rules archive](https://www.vekn.net/history-of-vtes-rules/436-rules-team-rulings-23-june-1998); canonical: [Google Groups](https://groups.google.com/g/rec.games.trading-cards.jyhad/c/tSpd9dtTElc/m/-CuHJF54_n0J); fetched 2026-07-22)
**Status:** current

> Blocking: "The last step of a successful block vs. a Ready acting minion is combat. Combat is not delayed until after the block. Thus, Cat's Guidance would be played after the combat, not before."
>
> Locations: "Changing the controller of a location has no other effect, unless specified by card text. Exception: A location "on" another controlled card is moved onto an appropriate card controlled by the new controller of the location."
>
> Weapon Damage: "a weapon's "current damage" is the amount of damage that the weapon would inflict if used as a strike by bearer against a generic opponent."
>
> Anathema: "Burns the target vampire when the target is reduced to zero blood in combat, regardless of the source of the loss of blood (card text). Still won't burn a vampire just for entering a combat with zero blood, however - there must be an actual "reduction" in blood."
>
> Brujah Frenzy: "Can only be played if the acting Brujah is Ready (not in Torpor)."

> Q [kschaefer]: You can play Relentless Reaper. The Relentless Reaper continuation of the current combat prevents
