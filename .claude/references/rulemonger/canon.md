### [RTR 20020501] — End-combat effects with after-combat riders fizzle when combat continues, except unlocks, which occur at strike resolution; no new combat while one is pending.
**Cited:** 21 footnotes (ch. 1, 3, 4, 5, 6)
**Filed under:** group "After Combat Ends" (G00071); group "Continue combat" (G00145); {Blissful Agony}; {Bond with the Mountain} (+36 more records)
**Source text:** original verbatim ([VEKN rules archive](https://www.vekn.net/history-of-vtes-rules/446-rules-team-rulings-1-may-2002); canonical: [Google Groups](https://groups.google.com/g/rec.games.trading-cards.jyhad/c/M1snoR2msbQ/m/RFwCmX3anDkJ); fetched 2026-07-22)
**Status:** current

> "The untap effect of MAJESTY (and Earth Meld and Meld with the Land) occurs when the strike resolves (it is not delayed until after combat ends)."
>
> "For allies that are able to play certain cards "as a vampire", they are treated as vampires for all parts of the resolution of the play of the appropriate cards. […] This reverses a few previous rulings regarding […] the ability of an ally to use Charming Lobby to call a vote (allowed if the ally plays Charming Lobby "as a vampire"). (REVERSAL)"
>
> "Effects that end combat and then do something else after combat (all in the single resolution of the effect) will fizzle if combat doesn't end or if a new combat is started. (Changes: Rotschreck followed by Fast Reaction or Psyche! will nullify the torpor effect.) Note that this doesn't apply to end combat and untap effects - the untap effect is not delayed to after combat (see Majesty ruling above)."
>
> "An effect which would cause (a new) combat cannot be used if there is already a "to be resolved later" combat."
>
> "Some cards grant a minion the option of doing something and then go on to say that some other effect will occur "if he does so". […] That is, "if he does so" means "if he uses this card's effect to do so". […] (REVERSAL)"

### [RTR 20010710] — Unpayable costs are paid as far as possible and the card or action resolves without effect; equipment-granted abilities are unusable while equipment use is barred.
**Cited:** 18 footnotes (ch. 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6)
**Filed under:** {Blood Brother Ambush}; {Blood of the Cobra}; {Drum of Xipe Totec}; {Hand of Conrad} (+9 more records)
**Source text:** original verbatim ([VEKN rules archive](https://www.vekn.net/history-of-vtes-rules/445-rules-team-rulings-11-july-2001); canonical: [Google Groups](https://groups.google.com/g/rec.games.trading-cards.jyhad/c/GsI1UyH54jU/m/x9LfDZX1vR4J); fetched 2026-07-22)
**Status:** current

> Q: "Speaking of Vast Wealth, there are disparate rulings for what happens when a peice of equipment is found that costs too much. […]"
> A: "Good question. In the interest of uniformity […] the partial cost ruling is reversed: You still cannot play a card or take an action whose cost cannot be paid. If, between the time you announce an action and the time the cost is paid, you are no longer able to pay the cost, then you pay as much of the cost as you can and, since the full cost is not paid, the action "fizzles" (resolves with no effect). If you are obliged to pay a cost that you cannot afford (via Vast Wealth or similar), then you pay as much of the cost as you can and the card whose cost you were paying has no effect (and goes to the ash heap)."
>
> Q: "Can a Blood Brother Ambush be played if combat was not about to occur (i.e., the acting Brujah antitribu was attempting to leave torpor)?"
> A: "Yes. In this case, the BBA-combat would negate the option of diablerizing the acting vampire; since it cancels combat, it cancels the diablerie option as well. (This latter part is a reversal of RTR 19970306.)"
>
> Q: "A vampire with no Potence equips with Hand of Conrad. In combat, his opponent plays Drawing Out the Beast. Can the vampire use Undead Strength?"
> A: "No. Anything he can do only by virtue of equipment he possesses is off limits to him while under the effects of Drawing Out the Beast."
>
> Q: "If I use Blood of the Cobra and play Dagon's Call as my strike at long range, is it still a hand strike?"
> A: "No. Dagon's call becomes "Strike: ranged. Make a hand strike ..." […] The modified Dagon's Call strike is not a hand strike, and the hand strike made as part of its effect is not effective at long range, although the rest of the Dagon's Call effect is."

### [RTR 19970630] — Damage is dealt by a minion only via strikes or explicit card text; other damage is environmental and sourceless; only vampires commit diablerie.
**Cited:** 17 footnotes (ch. 3, 4, 5, 6)
**Filed under:** group "Allies who can play as a vampire" (G00011); group "Action continues not specifically from block" (G00098); group "Set range" (G00127); group "Adding damage to strikes" (G00148) (+40 more records)

**Status:** current

> "If a minion plays a combat card that deals damage, the damage is considered dealt by that minion only if it results from a strike (including strike modifiers such as ammo cards) or if the effect specifically says the damage is dealt by the minion. Damage from other effects is considered "environmental," and has no particular source."
>

>

>
> "Storm Sewers sets the range for the combat and prevents anyone from choosing that combat's range, as well as eliminating the Establish Range step of combat."
>
> "Effects that increase the damage provided by strike cards affect those cards rather than the striking minion. For example, if a vampire plays Increased Strength followed by Burning Wrath, Increased Strength's effect boosts the aggravated damage dealt by Burning Wrath […]"

### [RTR 19980623] — Changing a location's controller has no side effects absent card text; combat is the final step of a successful block, preceding after-block reactions.
**Cited:** 12 footnotes (ch. 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6)
**Filed under:** group "Changes controller of location" (G00066); {Anathema}; {Brujah Frenzy}; {Cats' Guidance} (+9 more records)

**Status:** current

> Blocking: "The last step of a successful block vs. a Ready acting minion is combat. Combat is not delayed until after the block. Thus, Cat's Guidance would be played after the combat, not before."
>
> Locations: "Changing the controller of a location has no other effect, unless specified by card text. Exception: A location "on" another controlled card is moved onto an appropriate card controlled by the new controller of the location."

>
> Anathema: "Burns the target vampire when the target is reduced to zero blood in combat, regardless of the source of the loss of blood (card text). Still won't burn a vampire just for entering a combat with zero blood, however - there must be an actual "reduction" in blood."
>
> Brujah Frenzy: "Can only be played if the acting Brujah is Ready (not in Torpor)."

### [ANK 20210124] — An unblocked action whose targets became illegal, effects impossible, or cost unpayable fizzles; an action canceled mid-course counts as taken but never reaches resolution.
**Cited:** 9 footnotes (ch. 1, 3, 5)
**Filed under:** group "Action targeting locked minion" (G00078); group "Action targeting vampire in torpor" (G00080); group "Action targeting unlocked minion" (G00081); {Channel 10} (+8 more records)
**Source text:** original verbatim ([VEKN forum](https://www.vekn.net/forum/rules-questions/79001-the-question-of-the-month-action-fizzles#101492), fetched 2026-07-22)
**Status:** current

> "An action that fizzles is a successful action (= not blocked) whose effects cannot be applied because:
> 1/ the target (or ALL the targets) has become illegal during the course of the action
> 2/ things happened during the course of the action that prevent the action from happening
> 3/ the cost of the action cannot be paid anymore"
>
> "The action was not blocked, so it's successful. It fizzles because of 1/ (no valid target)."
>
