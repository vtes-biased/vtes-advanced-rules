## 1. Stealing the Edge after the holder burns it mid-action

**The question.** A minion announces an action to steal the Edge; the holder burns the
Edge during the action. Does the stealer still get it at resolution?

**The record.**

- "The action cannot be thwarted by burning the Edge after the action is announced."
  [RTR 19980623](https://groups.google.com/g/rec.games.trading-cards.jyhad/c/tSpd9dtTElc/m/-CuHJF54_n0J)
  — {Tereza Rostas}.
- "She cannot steal the Edge if it is uncontrolled."
  [TOM 19951214-2](https://groups.google.com/g/rec.games.trading-cards.jyhad/c/lVTQ1rstpLY/m/Dz0yYCdMHg8J)
  — announcement legality.

**The strain.** The RTR sentence admits two readings: (a) the stealer retrieves even a
burned, uncontrolled Edge at resolution; or (b) the holder simply has no standing way
to burn the Edge in response, so the "thwart" never materializes — and where a real
effect does burn it mid-action, the steal resolves with no effect like any vanished
object ([§3.4]). Owner lean (2026-07-22): reading (b) — the RTR is likely
misinterpreted by reading (a); TOM 19951214-2's announcement-legality rule and §3.4's
no-effect resolution point the same way.

The case is reachable: {Hand Intervention} ("That Methuselah may burn this card by
burning the Edge" — an in-play ability with no timing restriction, usable by its
target mid-action) and {Sabbat Threat} ("may burn the Edge to burn all the threat
counters he or she has") both let the holder burn the Edge during another
Methuselah's action.

**What a ruling would settle.** Whether a steal of the Edge announced while it was
controlled genuinely retrieves an Edge burned mid-action (reading (a)), or resolves
with no effect (reading (b), the owner's lean and [§3.4]'s general model).

**Where the document stands.** §6.5.4 carries the RTR sentence without the retrieval
consequence ("not thwarted by the holder burning it after announcement" — trimmed of
"the stealer still gets it at resolution", 2026-07-22).
