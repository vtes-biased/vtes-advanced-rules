### §4.3

One, and I did not write the opposite — I wrote the ruling and am reporting the collision.

§1.1: "A 'cannot' that names the cards it forbids bars playing them at all, including plays made only to cycle or for a side benefit. A 'cannot' that names an outcome leaves the play legal and only nullifies the outcome." (binds §4.3)

{Rigor Mortis} names an outcome, not cards: "The opposing minion cannot use any additional strikes this round." Applying §1.1's test literally, {Acrobatics} would remain playable for its dodge with the additional strike simply not usable. I do not think §1.1's decision is wrong as a general test — I think {Rigor Mortis} is a counterexample §1.1 has not accounted for, and someone should decide whether the test needs a carve-out for effects that bar a whole class of strike. I stated the ruling as written and cited it. Flagging rather than re-opening.

### §4.2

Three, none fatal, all resolved in favour of printed text.

1. R0663 {Fear of the Void Below} — the ruling says the card "affects the range immediately". Printed [dai] text is "This combat, each round DEFAULTS to long range", which is not a range-SET: a maneuver can still change it, so the card is not subject to the 4.2.1 exclusivity rule. Drafted as "made long the round's default", not "set". The reviewer note grouping it with the setters would have produced a false rule.

2. R2312 {Lucian, the Perfect} — the ruling's arithmetic does not reconcile cleanly with printed text. {Thrown Gate} costs 0; {Jann Berger} reduces [pot] cards by 1; {Lucian} adds 1 blood/life to strikes against him. I drafted that reading explicitly. It is inference, not quoted from the ruling; worth an owner check.

### §4.3

Two to flag, both handled by following the ruling and noting the wording.

1. R1487 {Rigor Mortis}.4 vs printed text. R0452 {Darkness Within}.1 says "The blood is removed during strike resolution, before mending damage." rulings.yaml phrasing invites the reading that the blood is available to mend; the printed card moves it TO THE CARD ("Move 1 blood or life from the opposing minion to this card"), not to the vampire, so it is not available to mend that round.
