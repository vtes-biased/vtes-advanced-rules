### 5.9 Traits and trait-change precedence

**Base rules.** Sect, clan, Black Hand membership, the acting-minion designation and the
like are *traits*: single-valued labels a minion carries, which other cards read as play
requirements, as conditions inside their own effect text, and as triggers. The rulebook
says only that a vampire belongs to one and only one sect and that a sect change replaces
the old value outright [RBK vampire-sects]. It gives no rule for two effects writing the
same trait, and none for what a trait-keyed effect does when the trait moves underneath
it. This section supplies both. Sect is the most heavily ruled trait, not the subject —
the rules below are trait-agnostic and 5.7, 5.8 and 5.1 defer to them for the traits they
name.

#### 5.9.1 Underlying value and overrides

Trait writes come in two shapes, and the distinction drives everything else.

- A **permanent change** rewrites the *underlying* value. Wording template: the effect is
  "a change of the actual sect" — the sect-changing actions and the rulebook action to
  become anarch, e.g. {Field Training}, {The Red Question}.
- A **temporary override** leaves the underlying value untouched and imposes a value above
  it for as long as the effect is in play. Wording template: the minion "is considered a
  \<trait\> vampire", e.g. {Writ of Acceptance}.

An override outranks the underlying value for the whole time it lasts, regardless of which
was established first. A permanent change made while an override is live therefore
succeeds — it writes the underlying value — but has no visible effect until the override
lapses; when it does, the minion surfaces at whatever the underlying value *then* is, not
at what it was when the override began. Correspondingly, an override is not disturbed by
an underlying change beneath it, whether that change is a card or the rulebook anarch
action.[^5-9-1] A card whose sole function was the permanent change may say it burns when
it is overridden; that burn is that card's own text, not part of the precedence rule —
cards carrying the same override clause without it, e.g. {Into the Fire}, simply lie
dormant.[^5-9-2]

A continuous effect that fixes a trait behaves as an override for this purpose and outlasts
underlying churn: {Tegyrius, Vizier}'s allegiance counter keeps its holder Camarilla
through any change to his underlying sect.[^5-9-1]

#### 5.9.2 The most recent write governs

Between two writes of the same kind on the same trait, the **later governs and supersedes
the earlier**; the earlier is not destroyed and resurfaces if the later lapses. This holds
on every trait for which it has been ruled — clan ({Derange} against {Clan Impersonation}),
the acting-minion designation ({Nar-Sheptha} against {Deep Song}), and sect — so state it
as the trait rule, not as a sect rule.[^5-9-3]

§2.4 governs only the ordering of *simultaneous* effects; this rule is about successive
ones, and the two never compete. §3.10 owns the acting-minion designation itself; it is
cited here only as proof the precedence rule is not sect-specific.

⚠ REVIEW — nothing rules on the unwinding of a *stack* of overrides: two overrides of the
same trait live at once, the earlier of which lapses first. The rules above cover only a
single override lapsing back onto the underlying value. Whether the surviving later
override simply continues (the reading the "later governs" rule suggests) needs judge
confirmation.

#### 5.9.3 Continuous effects that redirect a write

A continuous effect may constrain the *value* a later write produces rather than being
displaced by it. {Fall of the Camarilla} is the pattern: cards that would change a
vampire's sect to Camarilla remain playable and still resolve, but set the sect to
Independent while the Fall is in play; removing the Fall restores nothing retroactively,
the vampire simply reverts to his underlying sect. {Fall of the Sabbat} is the exact
mirror.[^5-9-4] Read such a card as a filter on writes, not as a competitor to them — it
does not lose to a more recent write, it edits its result.

#### 5.9.4 When the trait is read

**A trait-keyed effect tests the trait whenever it applies, not once when it was played.**
A continuous effect conditioned on a trait is therefore gained and lost freely as the trait
moves mid-action — including when the effect belongs to a minion other than the one whose
trait changed, e.g. {Ministry} and {Teresita, The Godmother} gaining or losing their
intercept as the acting vampire changes sect. The same rule read the other way fixes the
timing of a delayed test: {Warsaw Station}'s unlock is applied after the action and any
referendum resolve, so a vampire who is no longer Nosferatu at that moment does not
unlock. Effects that read a written trait likewise see its current value, respecting
earlier changes to it (e.g. {Powerbase: Savannah} and the acting-minion designation).[^5-9-5]

#### 5.9.5 Trait as requirement, as condition, and as action description

Three distinct things a card can do with a trait; keep them apart.

- **Requirement.** A current trait satisfies a requirement that reads it, however it was
  acquired. An impersonated or overridden sect is the vampire's sect for this purpose: he
  can play cards requiring that sect, and cards requiring one of its titles.[^5-9-6] (§5.8
  owns what happens to the title itself when the sect changes; §1.6 owns requirements
  generally.)
- **Condition inside effect text.** A trait named in a card's own effect is a condition on
  that effect, not a requirement to play or equip the card. {Stolen Police Cruiser} may be
  equipped by a non-Anarch and counts as a vehicle for him; only the clauses keyed to "this
  Anarch" lie dormant.[^5-9-7]
- **Action description.** Writing a trait, or playing a card that names it, does not make
  the action *an action described as* doing so. Playing {The Red Question} is not an action
  that "makes this vampire anarch" — {CrimethInc.} cannot be played on it — and equipping
  {Stolen Police Cruiser} is not an action requiring an Anarch.[^5-9-8] Cards keyed to an
  action's description read the description; cards keyed to a trait read the trait (§3.7.3).

#### 5.9.6 Independence of traits; minions with no trait

Traits are independent of one another. An effect that writes clan writes clan only —
{Clan Impersonation} and {Derange} change the target's clan and leave his sect
alone[^5-9-9] — and nothing infers one trait from another.

An ally has no sect and no clan, so no sect-keyed effect ever sees it, in either
direction: it neither benefits from nor is constrained by them. The common ally template
"may play cards requiring \<discipline\> as a \<sect\> vampire" (e.g. {Grey Thorne},
{Herald of Topheth}) is a card-play permission scoped to that clause, not a trait write —
such an ally is still not of that sect for anything else.[^5-9-10] See §5.5.
