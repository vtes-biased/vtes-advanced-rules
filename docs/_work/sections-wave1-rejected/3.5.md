### 3.5 Action repetition (NRA) and canceled actions

**Base rules.** A minion cannot perform an action with the same action card, from hand or
in play, more than once each turn, even if they unlock — the No Repeated Action (NRA)
rule. The limit is per minion, not per Methuselah: another minion may take the same
action. A canceled card has no effect but is still considered played; a canceled action
card does not lock the minion and may be played again.

Almost every ruling in this section answers one of two questions: **did the action reach
resolution** (which is what NRA keys on), and **is the window still open** (what may still
be played). They are independent axes, and the section is organized around keeping them
apart.

#### 3.5.1 Reaching resolution

An action reaches resolution when its outcome is determined: it succeeds, it is blocked,
or an effect makes it fail. Once resolution is reached, NRA binds the acting minion for
the rest of the turn, and ending the action afterwards does not undo that.[^3-5-1] This
covers the whole population of effects that end a blocked action before block resolution
(e.g. {Change of Target}, {Krassimir}) and every effect that makes an action fail (e.g.
{Champion}, {Secret Passage}) — a blocked action that failed cannot be attempted a second
time.[^3-5-2]

The mirror case is an effect that removes the action *before* its outcome is determined —
by canceling the action itself, or by canceling the block attempt that would have decided
it. The action never reached resolution, NRA never engaged, and the same vampire may
undertake the same action again (e.g. {Tangle Atropos' Hand}, {The Kiss of Ra}). The one
exception proves the test: if the action had already been blocked and was then continued
(e.g. [PRO] {Form of Mist}), resolution was already reached and no later undoing restores
the attempt.[^3-5-3]

An action that was undone after being taken was still *taken*. Effects that count actions
— e.g. {Enkil Cog}, [OBF] {Veil the Legions} — are consumed by it.[^3-5-4] This is where
the moment of cancellation matters: a card canceled as it is played (e.g. {Direct
Intervention}) was never an action at all and consumes nothing, whereas one undone after
play (e.g. {Tangle Atropos' Hand}) does. {Enkil Cog} shows both: canceled as played, the
vampire stays locked and may choose to act again or let the effect lapse; canceled
afterwards, the effect is spent.[^3-5-5]

#### 3.5.2 The sameness test

Three rules decide whether two actions are "the same" for NRA:

- **Rulebook actions are never the same as card-provided actions.** An action provided by
  the rulebook and an action provided by a card — played or in play — are different
  actions, whatever they do.[^3-5-6]
- **Rulebook actions differ by target.** They may be repeated against different targets.
  Equipping from a minion targets the equipment, so two such actions are the same if one
  equipment is the same.[^3-5-7]
- **Cards in play are distinguished by the card, not the name.** Actions provided by two
  different cards in play are different actions even when the cards share a name.[^3-5-8]

Two corollaries. The action taken to put a card into play and the action the card provides
once in play are different actions, so one vampire may take both in a turn if they unlock
in between (e.g. {Clandestine Contract}).[^3-5-9] And identity effects do not launder the
acting minion: changing a vampire's name or clan mid-action does not change *who is
acting* for NRA purposes (e.g. {Malleable Visage}).[^3-5-10] A genuinely new instance of a
minion is another matter — a retrieved ally or retainer, or a vampire restored by
{Soul Gem of Etrius}, is a new minion for all game purposes and is not bound by what its
previous incarnation did this turn.[^3-5-11]

Effects worded so that the action "does not count as performing" the action (e.g. {Piper},
{Muricia's Call}) leave NRA unengaged; §3.7.4 owns that wording template.

#### 3.5.3 Ended, failed, canceled

Three closure templates, and the difference between them is what may still be played.

**Ended.** "The action ends (unsuccessfully) immediately" closes the window at once: no
further action modifier or reaction may be played, and effects contingent on a later step
never occur (e.g. {Mirror Walk}, {Obedience}).[^3-5-12] The combat-flavoured variant is the
same rule read forward — no time remains for modifiers or reactions before combat begins
(e.g. {Brujah Frenzy}).[^3-5-13] Closing the window does not suppress effects that trigger
at the *same* moment; those still apply, ordered by the acting Methuselah.[^3-5-14]

**Failed.** An action made to fail has reached resolution, but the window is not slammed
shut: modifiers and reactions usable at the end of or after an action can still be played
(e.g. {Mistaken Identity}, {Detect Authority}).[^3-5-15] A failed action was not
*blocked*, however, so cards conditioned on a block cannot be played (e.g. {Mirror Image},
{Freak Drive}), and an action continued after being made to fail still fails.[^3-5-16]

**Canceled.** A canceled action is not performed. The minion does not lock and is free to
attempt the same action again, and costs and effects tied to the play are undone with it —
no burn for {Enrage}, no counters burned for a canceled {Corruption} action, no effect for
a card whose action was canceled.[^3-5-17] Where cancellation removes a *choice*, the
choice is remade and may be remade identically: a canceled strike still requires a strike
to be chosen, and the same strike may be chosen again.[^3-5-18]

Cancellation scope belongs to §1.8, but two limits recur here. A cancel-an-action effect
reaches only minion cards played from hand in the normal fashion — not a weapon played via
{Disguised Weapon} or an ally via {Piper}, and not the ally card itself — while within
that scope it reaches every action card type, including employ retainer, recruit ally and
political actions.[^3-5-19] And a canceled limited effect does not consume its limit, so
another may be used; the out-of-turn master limit is the standing exception, being spent by
the attempt whether or not the card was canceled.[^3-5-20]

#### 3.5.4 What still happens when the action stops

Effects keyed to the action *reaching resolution* apply even if the action then ends before
its consequences: damage from e.g. {Daring the Dawn} or {Force of Will} is applied when
the action reaches resolution even if it ends before combat or has no effect, and is
applied after combat if the action was blocked — but is not applied at all if the action
was canceled before resolution.[^3-5-21] On the same principle, a block that would have
been successful triggers its effects even though the action was ended before block
resolution (e.g. {Aching Beauty}, {Unleash Hell's Fury}).[^3-5-22]

Effects keyed to a *later* step are simply lost: simultaneous lock-and-enter-combat effects
and combat itself do not occur if the action ends first (e.g. {Deep Song}, {Siren's
Lure}).[^3-5-23] Costs already paid are not refunded — a card locked to enter combat stays
locked even if the action fails.[^3-5-24]

An action also stops if the acting minion stops being ready during it: the action ends and,
for a political action, no referendum is conducted. This holds whether the minion is left
unready after combat or leaves torpor mid-action, and the action cannot be
continued.[^3-5-25]

#### 3.5.5 Canceled blocks and canceled combats

A canceled block attempt is neither successful nor unsuccessful — it is simply canceled,
and neither outcome's consequences attach.[^3-5-26]

Canceling the *combat* is different: the action remains blocked. The blocking minion is
still locked, reaction cards pertaining to that combat can no longer be used, and
{Mask of a Thousand Faces} cannot be used to continue the action — but an effect that
continues the action as if unblocked (e.g. {Crypt's Sons}, {Momentary Delay}) still
works.[^3-5-27] §3.6 owns the continue-as-if-unblocked template.
