### 1.5 Abilities vs. cards

**Base rules.** A minion's printed ability, and an ability conferred by a card already in
play, is a property of a card in play. Using one is neither playing a card nor taking an
action. The rulebook constrains ability use in exactly one place: the "Lock X to do Y"
template, which requires the bearer unlocked [RBK wording-templates]. Everything else in
this section follows from those two facts.

#### 1.5.1 Ability use is not a card play

Costs and restrictions written against *playing a card* do not reach ability use.
{Safe Passage}'s [obe] additional cost is levied on cards played, so it does not apply to a
[POWER][REACTION]; {Secure Haven} charges nothing for using the effects of cards already in
play (e.g. {KRCG News Radio}).[^1-5-1]

This holds icon by icon. An ability printed with a [REACTION] icon does not count as playing
a reaction card, so effects that count, limit, or cancel reaction *cards* do not reach it —
but it is still usable only when reactions can be used, i.e. while unlocked or under a wake
effect (e.g. {Champion}, {Surge}). The requirement tracks the reaction *window*, not the
unlocked state as such: the Imbued conviction-powered form of the same template reads "when
reactions can be used (during an action)" (e.g. {Vigilance}).[^1-5-2]

Conferring an ability is not a wake. A card that lets a locked minion do something during an
action does not thereby let it play reaction cards (e.g. {No Secrets From the
Magaji}).[^1-5-3]

The converse of the first rule is the other half of the boundary: an effect keyed to *using
the card in play* does reach the abilities that card confers. A minion prevented from using
an equipment cannot use the disciplines or the maneuver that equipment provides (e.g.
{Drum of Xipe Totec} under {Drawing Out the Beast}).[^1-5-4]

Card plays stay on their own side of the line throughout. Where a card is meant to be
playable in a state that bars card play, its own text says so (e.g. {Kiss of Ra, The},
playable by an acting vampire in torpor); see §5.3.[^1-5-5]

#### 1.5.2 Locked and torpor do not bar ability use

Being locked is not a bar. An ability is usable while its bearer is locked unless it is a
"Lock X to do Y" effect [RBK wording-templates], or requires an action, a block, or the
reaction window (§1.5.1). This is the default, not a per-card grant: the many crypt and
library cards ruled "can be used while locked" restate the default rather than creating an
exception (e.g. {Extortion}, {Make an Example}).[^1-5-6] §5.2 owns the lock itself.

Torpor is likewise not a bar. Torpor removes acting (other than leave torpor), blocking, and
reaction cards [RBK torpor]; it does not suspend the vampire's printed abilities. An ability
requiring none of the barred things therefore works from torpor — including one that reaches
into a combat the torpid vampire is not in, or that hands out intercept (e.g. group "Ability
usable in torpor", {Mariel, Lady Thunder}).[^1-5-7]

⚠ REVIEW — a torpid vampire unlocks normally [RBK torpor] but takes no actions; whether it
may lock to use a "Lock X to do Y" ability is settled by no ruling. Every ruling pairing the
two states ("usable while locked or in torpor", e.g. {Montano}) is on an ability that
requires no lock, so the corpus does not answer it either way.

#### 1.5.3 Barred from acting is not barred from using abilities

A minion that may not act may still use its abilities, including lock-to-use ones, because
locking to use an ability is not an action. The decisive case is the recruited ally: an ally
cannot act the turn it is recruited [RBK recruit-ally], yet that same turn it may lock to use
a "lock this ally to…" ability (e.g. {Carlton Van Wyk}, {Underbridge Stray}).[^1-5-8]

§3.7.4 owns the recruit action itself; §5.5 and §5.6 own whether a minion entering play may
act. The rule here is the general one and is not confined to recruitment: read a prohibition
on *acting* as a prohibition on actions only.

#### 1.5.4 Reuse

Default: unlimited within the window. An ability may be used any number of times during a
single action unless its own wording limits it, and where there is a limit it is read off the
ability's own conditions rather than imposed generically — {Osric Vladislav} may use his
repeatedly in one action, but only while he is acting and only if stealth is needed (e.g.
also {Corpse Minion}, {General Perfidio Díos}).[^1-5-9]

Two things narrow that default:

- **A lock in the cost.** A "Lock X to do Y" ability is self-limiting at one use per unlock;
  reuse requires an unlocking effect (e.g. {Forest of Shadows}, {Hukros}).[^1-5-10]
- **A named window.** "During X, do Y" allows one use per X [RBK wording-templates], so extra
  windows give extra uses: a second master phase action or a second discard phase action each
  carries its own use (e.g. {Nahir}, {Josef von Bauren}).[^1-5-11]

An ability spent on a payment that never happens is not spent. If the cost cannot be met even
with the ability, or the card it was used for is cancelled and no cost is paid, the ability
counts as unused for that action or turn even though its use was announced (e.g.
{Nergal}).[^1-5-12] See §1.7 for costs, §1.8 for cancellation.

#### 1.5.5 Timing, and abilities that require the bearer to participate

An ability that duplicates a card type is timed as that card type. An ability increasing
bleed is an action modifier — usable at any point during the action, including after block
attempts have concluded (e.g. {Pentex(TM) Loves You!}, {Spiridonas}).[^1-5-13]

An ability whose text speaks of combat requires its bearer to be a combatant, not merely that
a combat is occurring (e.g. {Watenda}, {White Lily}).[^1-5-14] The general form: an ability
naming a game state its bearer can occupy is read as requiring the bearer to occupy it, not
as triggering on any instance of that state. See §4.1.
