### 5.5 Allies

**Base rules.** An ally is a minion that is not a vampire. It enters play with its
starting life, is burned when it loses its last life, and treats aggravated damage as
normal damage. Some allies may play designated cards "as a vampire": for the effect
generated by that play only, the ally counts as a vampire of capacity 1, its life stands
in for blood, and a result that would send it to torpor burns it instead.

#### 5.5.1 Life is not blood

An ally has life, not blood. It therefore cannot pay a blood cost, and an effect whose
text takes blood *from* its subject cannot use an ally as that subject.[^5-5-1] The bar is
symmetric: a card that names blood on the target — the {Taste of Vitae} / {Enhanced
Coagulant} template — simply has no legal target in an ally and cannot be played against
one, and a card that inflicts a blood burn as a rider does nothing to an ally, e.g.
{Shackles of Enkidu}.[^5-5-2] This is the named-object case, not the futility case; §1.6
owns the boundary between a requirement to play and a play that merely accomplishes
nothing.

Transfers between the two pools convert on arrival: blood moved to an ally becomes life,
life moved to a vampire becomes blood, e.g. {Blood Turnip}.[^5-5-3]

The bar is a *cost* bar, and it is separable from the *requirement* bar. An effect that
pays or waives the cost lets an ally through where nothing else about the card demands a
vampire — allies with blood costs are kept by paying the pool cost alone under
{Kindred Segregation}, and {The Shard, London} lets allies play blood-cost cards that do
not otherwise require a clan or Discipline.[^5-5-4] Conversely, an ally still cannot use
an action or ability whose stated cost is blood, e.g. {Codex of the Edenic
Groundskeepers}.[^5-5-1]

#### 5.5.2 Starting life

"Starting life" is a fixed trait recorded at entry, not a cap and not a synonym for
current life. An ally's life total may exceed its starting life, and excess life does not
drain off.[^5-5-5] The trait survives transformation of the minion and is read from what
the card entered play as, not from what it later became: {Ghouled} recruits a mortal and
ghouls it afterwards, so the mortal's life figure is the starting life, and retainers
converted to allies keep their counters and their original starting life while losing
their abilities and any continuous effect on them.[^5-5-6] A minion that acquires ally
status without an ally card underneath it has no starting-life trait at all, so effects
keyed to it do nothing — e.g. a vampire treated as an ally by {The Grandest Trick} cannot
be given life by {Vagabond Mystic}.[^5-5-7]

#### 5.5.3 Torpor and burning

Allies do not go to torpor. Any effect that would send an ally to torpor burns it
instead, whichever direction the effect comes from — the ally's own play of a card "as a
vampire", a card played on it, or a combat result.[^5-5-8] Aggravated damage is not such
an effect: it burns life like normal damage and does not by itself remove the ally.[^5-5-9]
Effects that name torpor as their own consequence resolve on the vampire and leave the
ally untouched, e.g. {Legacy of Power} used against an ally.[^5-5-10]

A card is an ally only while it is in play. In the ash heap it is an action card, not an
ally, and ally-targeting effects cannot reach it.[^5-5-11] Likewise, flavor in an ally's
name or text does not create a card trait: {Gargoyle Slave} is neither a Gargoyle nor a
slave for game purposes.[^5-5-11]

#### 5.5.4 "As a vampire"

The phrase grants permission to play a card, and applies the card's own effect to the
ally on vampire terms. **It does not make the ally a vampire.** Effects that key off
being a vampire, or that act on the ally from outside that one play, still see an ally —
e.g. {Veil of Darkness} does not affect an ally playing a Discipline card "as a vampire",
and {The Line} cannot be used by one.[^5-5-12] The same reading applies to the mirror
clause: "block as an ally" alters only the who-may-block check and does not strip the
blocker of anything else, so a vampire blocking "as an ally" still plays Discipline
cards normally.[^5-5-13] Read every "as a …" clause to exactly the extent of what it says.

Within that scope the permission is broad. An ally acting "as a vampire" may play *any*
card the clause covers, not only Discipline-requiring cards (a reversal of earlier
rulings), e.g. {Shadow Court Satyr} playing {Taste of Vitae} — which it may play but may
never be the target of (§5.5.1).[^5-5-14] It may take directed actions, which it otherwise
cannot,[^5-5-15] and it may call a referendum listed on a political action card in hand,
e.g. {Herald of Topheth} with {Charming Lobby}.[^5-5-16] It may not commit diablerie by any
route.[^5-5-17]

Because life is the ally's blood, spending life is a real cost with a real floor. An ally
that spends its last life to play a card is burned at once and the play is orphaned: an
action modifier so paid causes the action to fail, and a strike so paid ends combat before
strike resolution.[^5-5-18]

A vampire pushed the other way — treated as an ally — is treated as an ally and nothing
more. It pays blood costs with life and keeps abilities it already had, including "as if
he had" Discipline abilities, which it then exercises on ally terms.[^5-5-19]

#### 5.5.5 Discipline level

An ally that carries a Discipline carries exactly one level of it, fixed at entry: the
level used to recruit it decides whether it is the inferior or the superior version, and
that never changes afterwards.[^5-5-20] It follows that such an ally cannot use an effect
requiring two or more Disciplines, e.g. {Shadow Court Satyr}.[^5-5-21]

#### 5.5.6 Entering play and acting

An ally recruited with the recruit-ally action cannot act that turn. An ally that enters
play by any other route — a card that puts it into play directly, a search effect, a
summon — is a ready unlocked minion immediately and **may act that same turn**.[^5-5-22]
Recruiting an ally and separately playing that ally from hand are distinct actions, so a
minion that unlocks may do both in the same turn, e.g. {The Summoning}.[^5-5-23] An ally
can be the acting minion in its own right, including in combat.[^5-5-24]

An on-entry consequence written into the ally's own text is *not* a requirement to play,
and so is not skipped by an abnormal entry route. {War Ghoul}'s burn of another ally or
retainer applies however it came into play, including through {Pressing Flesh}; if nothing
is available to burn, the War Ghoul itself is burned.[^5-5-25] The check is made as each
ally arrives, so an effect that brings several allies at once lets the controller fix the
order and thereby the victim.[^5-5-26] (§1.6 states the general rule that abnormal entry
ignores a card's *requirements*; this is the on-entry-effect exception to how far that
reaches.) By the same token, "recruited" in another card's trigger reads as "came into
play as an ally" and covers non-standard routes.[^5-5-25]
