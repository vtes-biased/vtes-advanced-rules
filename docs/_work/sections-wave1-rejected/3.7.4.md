### 3.7.4 Employ retainer & recruit ally

**Base rules.** Both are undirected +1 stealth actions available to any ready minion, paying
the cost printed on the ally or retainer card; on success the retainer is placed on the
acting minion and the ally in your ready region. The rulebook also fixes the abnormal case:
a card put into play "by some means other than employing/recruiting" uses its basic version,
and an ally brought into play by other means may act the turn it arrives.
[RBK employ-retainer] [RBK recruit-ally]

This section owns the two actions and, more importantly, the seam between them and **entry
into play by a card effect**. It does not own what the arriving minion may then do: whether
it may use its abilities the turn it arrives is §1.5 (locking as an ability cost is not
acting — §5.2), and whether it may act is §5.5 for allies and §5.6 for retainers.

#### 3.7.4.1 The action itself

An employ retainer or recruit ally action is an ordinary action, blockable and cancellable
by any effect that cancels an action card — such effects reach employ, recruit and political
actions alike. Where one card can bring in either an equipment or a retainer (the "this
vampire equips or employs … from your hand" template, e.g. {Jack of Both Sides}), the action
takes its type from the card actually brought in, so equip-keyed and employ-keyed effects
read the outcome rather than the enabling card.[^3-7-4-1] See §3.7.3 for the equip action.

Two timing points follow from the card being *played* as an action rather than simply
appearing:

- **Replacement precedes entry.** A card recruited or employed the normal way has been
  replaced by the time the minion enters play, so an entering minion's own "after this enters
  play" ability sees a refilled hand. Put into play by an effect instead, it has not been
  replaced, and the ability must work with the hand and ash heap as they stand
  (§1.9).[^3-7-4-2]
- **Traits are read as printed, then modified.** An effect riding on the announcement that
  changes what the arriving minion *is* takes hold only after the recruitment resolves: the
  card is recruited as the type its card prints, cost reductions keyed to that printed type
  apply, and its starting life is the base card's — the {Ghouled} template.[^3-7-4-2]

A printed prohibition on equipping or employing is read as a bar on **acquisition by any
route**, not merely on announcing the action: a minion who "cannot have or use equipment or
retainers" can neither attempt the action nor strike to steal one, e.g. {The Beast, The
Leatherface of Detroit}.[^3-7-4-3]

#### 3.7.4.2 Entry into play by a card effect

Many cards put an ally or retainer into play without a recruit ally or employ retainer
action. Two wording templates recur, and the difference between them is load-bearing:

- **Recruiter-designating:** "*This vampire / that Anarch recruits (employs) … from your
  hand (requirements and cost apply as normal)*" — e.g. {Piper}, {The Summoning} at [PRE],
  {Pack Alpha}. A minion recruits or employs, so the card's own requirements must be met and
  that minion's disciplines are read the normal way for discipline-graded text.
- **Bare put-into-play:** "*put that card in play*" — e.g. {Summon History}. Nobody recruits
  or employs; the card's requirements do not apply and the rulebook's basic-version default
  governs discipline-graded text (§1.6 owns requirements under abnormal entry).[^3-7-4-4]

In **neither** case does the ally or retainer's own requirement or cost become a requirement
or cost *of the action being taken*. An effect that reads the action's requirements — e.g.
{CrimethInc.}, usable after a successful action requiring an Anarch — does not see an
Anarch requirement printed on the ally that action fetched.[^3-7-4-4]

**Not an action.** Where the enabling effect is not itself an action ({Piper}, {Pack Alpha},
{Zhenga}'s extra recruits, a card that matures into play from the uncontrolled region), no
action occurs at all. Effects keyed to an action therefore do not attach: cost reductions
naming the action ("recruit ally actions cost this vampire -1", e.g. {Charisma}), triggers
keyed to announcing one ({Zhenga} does not fire off {Piper}), and stealth modifiers keyed to
"any action to put a … in play" ({Blood Cult Awareness Network} gives no -1 stealth to a
{Web of Knives Recruit} completing its training).[^3-7-4-5]

**Not played.** However the effect is worded, the ally or retainer card is not *played* by
the minion. No cancel-a-played-card window opens on it, so cancel effects — whether keyed to
playing ({Louhi}) or to the normal way of playing ({Direct Intervention}) — cannot reach
it.[^3-7-4-6] §1.8 owns the cancel window.

**The No Repeated Action rule is not engaged.** Bringing an ally or retainer in by effect is
not performing the recruit or employ action, so the same minion may still play that same card
from hand normally later in the turn if it unlocks. This holds even when the enabling card is
itself printed as an employ retainer action ({Muricia's Call}) — the NRA compares card names,
not effects, and the enabling card is a different name.[^3-7-4-7] §3.5 owns sameness.

**What does attach.** The counterpole, and the practical rule: an effect keyed to the
recruit/employ **event**, to the **minion** doing it, or to the **card** rather than to the
action applies however the ally or retainer arrived. Cost reductions of the "allies and
retainers requiring X cost this minion -1" and "reduce the cost of recruiting or employing …"
templates apply to non-standard recruitment (e.g. {Little Tailor of Prague}, {Soul of the
Earth}), as do abilities keyed to "when she recruits or employs" ({Baba Yaga, the Iron Hag});
and a reduction naming the *action card* rather than the action applies to a card that is not
played as an action at all ({The Line} on a retainer employed with {Pack
Alpha}).[^3-7-4-8]

So the test is not whether an action occurred but **what the modifying effect names**: name
the action and it lapses on effect-entry; name the card, the minion or the recruit/employ
event and it survives.
