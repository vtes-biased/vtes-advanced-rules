### 1.8 Playing and canceling a card

**Base rules.** A card is played by announcing its effects, showing it, and placing it in
the ash heap (or the play area) on resolution. Only cancellation effects and wake effects
may be used during the "as played" window of another card; even drawing to replace comes
after it. A canceled card has no effect but is still considered played: an action card so
canceled does not lock the minion and is not paid for, while a non-action card's cost is
paid as normal.

#### 1.8.1 The permissive default

**A card may be played, or an action taken, knowing it will accomplish nothing.** Futility
is never in itself a bar: a card with no effect is still a card played, and the play is
legal.[^1-8-1] What bars a play is a *requirement*, not an absence of effect — a card whose
text names the object it acts on cannot be played when no such object exists. That boundary
is §1.6's to draw; do not infer a prohibition from the mere fact that nothing will happen.
Whether a no-effect action nonetheless resolves successfully, and whether it locks the
actor, is §3.4's.

#### 1.8.2 The "as played" window

The window admits cancellation effects and wakes, and nothing else. Cancellation must be
played immediately; no other effect may be interposed, and no card is replaced inside the
window.[^1-8-2] A wake may be used there, but a reaction to "unlock and attempt to block"
may not: the permission is to wake, not to react.[^1-8-2] The window is when an opponent
gets to wake in response to a card being played at all — before its effects apply (e.g.
{Psalm of the Damned}, {Deed the Heart's Desire}).[^1-8-3]

The bar on other effects reaches library-search and draw masters specifically: such a card
cannot be used inside the window to fetch the cancel card you want to play there (e.g. {The
Barrens}, {Dreams of the Sphinx}).[^1-8-4]

When a canceling *ability* is used, its effect is applied before anyone has the opportunity
to play a further cancellation effect against it (e.g. {Veil of Darkness}, {Andrew
Stuart}).[^1-8-5] ⚠ REVIEW — this is the only evidence on cancellation priority in the
corpus, and both instances are card abilities. Nothing rules on canceling a cancel *card*,
nor on the order in which competing Methuselahs may cancel; do not generalize the above
into a priority rule without a judge ruling.

#### 1.8.3 What can be canceled

**Only a card played from hand in the normal fashion.** A card put into play by another
card's effect was never played, so no as-played window opens for it and no cancellation
reaches it. This holds identically for masters, for minion cards, and for allies brought
into play by an effect (e.g. weapons via {Disguised Weapon}, allies via {Piper} or
{Zhenga}).[^1-8-6] The same is true of a card used through a host: using the ability of a
card placed on a minion "as if played from your hand" is not playing that card, so it cannot
be canceled — except where playing the card is itself part of the effect being resolved.
The mirror consequence matters as much: because the host card was not played by the minion,
it does not consume that minion's own once-per-round or once-per-action limits, and the
minion may still use the same modifier himself.[^1-8-7]

Within that limit, the window is broad. It exists for every action-card type, including
employ retainer, recruit ally and political action, and for out-of-turn master cards.[^1-8-8]
It exists for cards played at the end of a round and for cards played "when combat would
end" — a cancellation played there counts as having been played during that ending round —
and for action modifiers played "after the action or referendum is successful" (e.g.
{Botched Move}, {Death Seeker}, {Ophidian Gaze}).[^1-8-9]

A canceler conditioned on a Discipline reaches only cards actually played *with* that
Discipline, not cards that could have been played with it (e.g. {Hide the Mind} against
{Anesthetic Touch} played with [obe]).[^1-8-10]

**When a card counts as played decides whether a window exists at all.** A political action
card put into play from hand by an effect that treats it as played only at successful
resolution never has an as-played window, so it cannot be canceled as played (e.g. {Charming
Lobby}); where the retained card is instead played normally, requirements must be met and it
is cancelable as played (e.g. {Echo of Harmonies}).[^1-8-11] State the timing rule, not two
card exceptions.

#### 1.8.4 A canceled card has still been played

Every consequence in this subsection follows from that one sentence.

- The same reaction or action modifier cannot be played again by that minion, and any card
  whose text forbids playing it more than once in the round is spent (e.g. {Immortal
  Grapple}).[^1-8-12]
- Any effect that counts or reacts to cards played counts the canceled one — a counter is
  added, a "no reaction cards were played" condition is broken, a retrieval effect can
  retrieve it (e.g. {Infernal Familiar}, {Perfectionist}, {Marthe Dizier}).[^1-8-13]
- But the *limit on the effect* is not consumed. Canceling a limited effect — an additional
  bleed, an additional strike — does not trigger the limit, and another limited effect of
  the same kind may still be used.[^1-8-14] The card is spent; the effect budget is not.
- A canceled trifle grants no additional master phase action, since the trifle was not
  successfully played.[^1-8-15] Contrast the printed rule for out-of-turn masters, which
  count against your next master phase even when canceled.

**Costs.** A canceled non-action card's cost is still paid, and this is the rule the
cost-carrying wording templates all resolve to: the blood cost of a canceled strike modifier
(e.g. the {Target} cycle), the pool bid or counters of a canceled change-of-control effect,
the price of a canceling ability itself.[^1-8-16] The exception is printed, not derived —
some cancelers state that the canceled card's cost is not paid ({Hide the Mind}). A canceled
*action* card is the other case named in the rulebook: it is not paid for, the minion does
not lock, and the same action may be attempted again. §1.7 owns cost arithmetic.

**Enabling resources.** A resource that supplies a *requirement* is spent on announcement:
the card was played, so the requirement was used, even though it was canceled (e.g. {Vidal
Jarbeaux}). An *optional cost modifier* is spent on payment: if the card is canceled and no
cost is paid, the modifier was not used and remains available (e.g. {Nergal}).[^1-8-17]

**Replacement.** Nothing is replaced inside the window (§1.8.2), and replacement is §1.9's
subject; two points belong here. A "do not replace until" clause — or an alternate
replacement clause such as {Steely Tenacity}'s — is canceled along with the card that
carried it, and the card is then replaced normally. A canceled card that would itself have
drawn cards draws none: only the one replacement card comes.[^1-8-18]

#### 1.8.5 Canceling part of an effect

Cancellation reaches the component named, and only that component.

- Canceling a strike means the striking minion chooses another strike, which may come from
  another strike card; anything the canceled strike carried goes with it. Canceling a
  *maneuver* provided by a strike card does not cancel the strike, and the chosen strike
  cannot be changed — but a press provided by that maneuver does fall with it. Canceling a
  strike modifier that redirects the strike leaves the strike resolving on its default
  target (e.g. {Rigor Mortis}, {Target Retainer}).[^1-8-19]
- Where a card cancels one named effect of a multi-effect card, the rest resolves: canceling
  a change of control prevents the control change only, and counters already burned stay
  burned.[^1-8-20]

Cancellation *as a card is played* is this section's subject. Cancellation of an action
*after* the card has been played — where the action still counts as one of the actions
taken — is a different mechanism and belongs to §3.5.[^1-8-21]
