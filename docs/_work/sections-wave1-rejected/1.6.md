### 1.6 Requirements

**Base rules.** A requirement is a printed prerequisite for *playing* a card: the clan or
Discipline icon on the attribute bar, and any further prerequisite stated in the card text
(capacity, title, sect, trait, "requires a ready Ⓐ…"). A minion card can be played only by a
minion who meets its requirements; a master card only by a Methuselah controlling a ready
minion who meets them [RBK requirements-for-playing-cards]. Everything below turns on that
word *playing*.

#### 1.6.1 Requirements are checked when the card is played, and only then

A requirement is a gate on the play, not a condition of continued existence. It is measured
once, at the moment the card is played; nothing re-checks it afterwards, and a card already in
play is unaffected when the requirement stops being met.[^1-6-1] The corollary is that a card
played again must meet it again, so an effect that itself destroys the requirement makes the
second play illegal, e.g. {Undead Persistence} in the same combat.[^1-6-2] Within a single
play the measurement is likewise fixed at announcement: a later change to the acting minion
does not retroactively invalidate a card already played, e.g. {Mental Maze} after a slave
replaces the acting vampire.[^1-6-3]

Because the check happens at play, a burn option or other use printed on a card in play remains
available to a minion who met the requirement when the card was played but no longer does.[^1-6-4]

Two things that look like plays are not:

- **Taking control of a card already in play** — requirements are not checked, because nothing
  is being played. §6.3 owns this; do not read the twelve cards carrying that boilerplate as a
  requirements rule.[^1-6-5]
- **Equipping from another minion you control** (the rulebook equip action, no cost) — the
  equipment is already in play, so a minion who does not meet its requirements may take it,
  e.g. clan equipment.[^1-6-6]

A requirement satisfied later is satisfied for cards already played: a capacity gain validates
cards played before it, e.g. {Orun} and {Aranthebes, The Immortal}.[^1-6-7] ⚠ REVIEW — the
converse (a capacity or Discipline *reduction* after a card requiring it is in play) is
unevidenced; do not assume symmetry.

#### 1.6.2 Requirement to play vs. restriction on use

Distinguish two wording templates that both name a trait:

- **A requirement** gates the play. It is checked once (§1.6.1) and, once passed, the card
  functions in full.
- **"Only usable by X"** on a card that goes into play gates the *use* of the abilities that
  card grants, not the play that put it there. A minion who fails it may play and keep the
  card, its passive effects still apply, and only the abilities conditioned on the trait are
  unavailable — e.g. {Seal of Veddartha} equipped by a capacity-5 vampire grants its Discipline
  levels but cannot be used to bleed; {The Crusader Sword} can be equipped by a minion who
  cannot strike with it.[^1-6-8]

The same reading applies to a trait clause anywhere in a card in play: the clause it gates
fails, the rest of the card does not. A restriction on the card's *subject* — "put this card on
a Red List Anarch" — is a targeting restriction, not a requirement, with consequences in
§1.6.5.[^1-6-9] Costs are not requirements either and are checked separately; see §1.7.

#### 1.6.3 Abnormal entry: putting a card into play is not playing it

When an effect puts a card into play rather than having it played — diablerie, {Concealed
Weapon}, {Piper}, {The Summoning}, {Alastor}, a vampire's special ability — the card's
requirements are not checked at all, and the card then functions normally once in play.[^1-6-10]
Where a Discipline requirement is bypassed the basic version of the card is used
[RBK equip]; the same holds for allies and retainers brought in this way, and a variable cost X
is zero.[^1-6-11] §1.7 owns the cost side.

Conversely, an effect that has you *play* a card applies its requirements in the ordinary way,
whatever route the card took to your hand or to the moment of play, e.g. a political action
retained by {Echo of Harmonies}, or a retainer employed through {Pack Alpha}.[^1-6-12]

**What abnormal entry does not bypass.** Skipping requirements is not skipping everything:

- **Prohibitions.** A "cannot have" restriction on the recipient is not a requirement and is not
  bypassed: an equipment cannot be placed on a minion barred from equipment, nor a second copy
  on a minion already carrying one.[^1-6-13] Nor can an effect that plays or mirrors another card
  be used to escape a play restriction.[^1-6-14]
- **Legality on arrival.** A card put on a minion who cannot legally bear it is burned — with no
  cost refunded, and this includes the case where the disqualifier is the card's own requirement,
  e.g. a weapon retrieved by {Horrid Reality}.[^1-6-15] Effects that fired on placement still
  apply even though the card is burned, e.g. {Wooden Stake}'s "does not unlock as normal"; and a
  card that harms the minion it sits on does so however it got there, including by diablerie or
  theft.[^1-6-16]
- **On-entry costs.** A clause that fires when the card comes into play fires regardless of how
  it came into play, e.g. {War Ghoul}'s burn cost through {Pressing Flesh}.[^1-6-17]

**Equipped vs. merely put into play.** A clause conditioned on the *equip* does not fire when the
card is only put into play; a clause conditioned on the card being in play does, e.g. {Shilmulo
Tarot} put on with {Alastor} draws no cards but does when equipped with {Magic of the Smith};
{Helicopter} arrives locked only if equipped.[^1-6-18] Symmetrically, a minion ability keyed to
equipping or to recruiting applies to non-standard equips and recruits, e.g. {Topaz}, {Little
Tailor of Prague} — but an ability keyed to an *action* does not, since abnormal entry involves
no action, e.g. {Zhenga} with {Piper}.[^1-6-19]

Finally, the requirements and cost of a card fetched or put into play by another card are not
requirements or costs *of that other card*: an effect keyed to the action card's requirements
does not see the ally's, e.g. {The Summoning} and {CrimethInc.}.[^1-6-20]

#### 1.6.4 Requirement vs. futility — the boundary

Two rules are routinely conflated. State them separately.

**Futility is never a bar.** A card may be played, and an action taken, in the knowledge that it
will accomplish nothing: a target with none of the resource the card removes is still a legal
target, a bonus that is not needed may still be played, a dodge may be played against a strike it
cannot avoid. There is no general "a card must do something" rule to invoke. §1.8 owns the
permissive default for playing cards; §3.4 owns the separate question of whether a no-effect
action still resolves successfully and still locks the actor.

**The bar is the card's own text, and it is a requirement like any other.** A card cannot be
played when either limb fails:

1. **No legal object.** The card's text names a thing it acts on and no such thing exists — no
   damage for a prevention card, no equipment to destroy, nothing in an ash heap to remove,
   e.g. {Soak}, {Shattering Blow}, {Vulture's Buffet}.[^1-6-21]
2. **An instruction that cannot be carried out in full.** The card mandates something the game
   state does not permit — putting a card from hand under {Storage Annex} when it is the only
   card in hand, burning seven library cards with fewer than seven, allocating points among
   subjects that do not exist.[^1-6-22]

The discriminator is *the object of a mandatory instruction*, not *the size of the result*. If
the card can identify everything its text names and can carry out what it mandates, it is
playable however pointless the outcome — a vampire with no blood is still a vampire whose blood
the card names. If it cannot, the play is illegal, and it is illegal for the same reason a
clanless vampire cannot play a clan card: an unmet requirement, checked at the moment of play
under §1.6.1. This is a play-legality question, not a resolution question; a card legally played
that turns out to change nothing has still been played.

#### 1.6.5 Abilities that meet requirements without possessing the trait

A family of vampires (e.g. {Mata Hari}, {Vidal Jarbeaux}) can be treated as meeting a clan, sect,
title or trait requirement they do not have. One rule governs the whole family:

> The ability substitutes for the requirement **for all effects of the card played, and only
> them, including its duration effects. It does not make the vampire count as such for what the
> card does from being in play.**[^1-6-23]

So {Houngan} may be played on a vampire who fakes the required clan, but he gets no blood from
it and cannot lock it for blood; a contract that chooses him *as it is played* has chosen him for
other cards, while a contract whose choice is an effect of the card in play has not.[^1-6-24]

The rest follows from that sentence plus §1.6.1 and §1.6.3:

- **No play, nothing to fake.** The ability does nothing for a card not played normally — an
  equipment placed by {Concealed Weapon}, an ally recruited by {Piper} or {The Summoning} — because
  no requirement is being checked.[^1-6-25]
- **No requirement, nothing to fake.** Where the card states a restriction on its subject rather
  than a requirement to play, the ability is irrelevant, e.g. {Infamous Insurgent}'s "Red List
  Anarch", {Sacrifice}'s clan matching (§1.6.2).[^1-6-26]
- **Meeting a requirement is not possessing the trait.** The vampire does not acquire the
  Discipline, clan, sect or title for any other purpose: he gets no benefit from cards that
  merely reference the trait, is not seen as holding the title by other cards during the
  referendum, and gets no votes from a faked title.[^1-6-27] He may, however, use the effects of a
  card whose play the faked trait gated — that is the same card, still within "all effects of the
  card played".[^1-6-28]
- **Scope is whatever the ability's own text says**; some reach any card including master cards,
  others only certain minion cards.[^1-6-29]
- **Only while in play.** The ability does not apply to the vampire in the crypt or the
  uncontrolled region, so a card fetching by the faked trait cannot find him.[^1-6-30]
- **Burn options** printed on a card played through a faked requirement stay usable (§1.6.1).[^1-6-4]
- A card **canceled as it is played** was still played, so the requirement counts as having been
  used; see §1.8.[^1-6-31]

**Titles.** Meeting a title requirement implies membership of the underlying sect, and works even
if that sect no longer exists. The controller chooses how the title is met (any city, even an
invented one) but may meet each given title only once; a card requiring merely a "titled" vampire
forces a choice of some title, under the same once-each limit.[^1-6-32]

#### 1.6.6 Reading requirement lines

- **Clan pairs.** A requirement naming a clan and its antitribu is met by either, not
  both.[^1-6-33]
- **Traits that imply others.** A card requiring a Baron is a card requiring an Anarch — and,
  conversely, a Baron is not thereby able to play cards requiring a Baron by any other route than
  the printed one.[^1-6-34]
- **Multi-Discipline requirements.** An ability granting a Discipline for the purpose of meeting
  requirements meets one of a card's Discipline requirements, not both, e.g. {Infernal Familiar},
  {Grey Thorne}.[^1-6-35]
- **Split requirements.** Where a card lets one minion play it for another's benefit, the
  requirements divide as the card states: the player must meet only what playing the card
  demands, the recipient must meet everything else, e.g. {Inscription}.[^1-6-36]
