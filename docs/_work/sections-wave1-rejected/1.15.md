### 1.15 Cumulative and stacking effects

This section governs what happens once *several instances of an effect exist at the same
time* — several copies of a card in play, several counters of one kind on a minion, or
two different cards granting a similar benefit. It does not govern how often a single
effect may be used: periodicity and per-action limits are §1.2, and the scoping of
"once per action" limited effects stays there.

#### 1.15.1 The default is cumulation

Each copy of a card in play is a separate object. It exerts its own effect, imposes its
own cost, and is satisfied or removed on its own. There is no merging of copies and no
implicit cap: absent wording to the contrary, two copies do twice the work and demand
twice the payment.[^1-15-1]

- Repeated pool- or blood-burning requirements are cumulative, whether they come from
  several copies of one card or from several different sources — e.g. {Anarch Revolt},
  {First Tradition: The Masquerade} (two copies burn 4 pool, not 4 total shared).[^1-15-1]
- The cost is charged **per copy**, not per target and not per controller. {Betrayer} is
  the canonical case: a Methuselah facing two of them pays 1 pool for each, even where the
  same vampire is the betrayer in both.[^1-15-1]
- Counters and pool are paid per copy, but a **single game event** that a copy's text keys
  on satisfies every copy keyed on it — the controller of two {First Tradition: The
  Masquerade} who skips his turn satisfies both.[^1-15-1]

#### 1.15.2 Copies resolve independently

Independence follows from cumulation and applies to resolution, not only to cost.
Each copy is targeted, triggered, moved and removed on its own; nothing about one copy's
resolution disposes of another.[^1-15-2]

- Copies resolve in succession rather than as one combined effect, and may repeat a choice
  the earlier copy already made — e.g. {Courier}, {Camarilla Exemplary} (the same vampire
  may be chosen by each copy).[^1-15-2]
- A copy in play keeps its own counters and its own state. Removing the host does not
  dispose of the others: stealing a minion carrying two {Temptation} burns the counters on
  one of them only.[^1-15-2]
- A per-copy cap is lifted by another copy, never by repeating the triggering event.
  {Scorn of Adonis} costs a given Methuselah one pool however many "no" votes he cast; a
  second {Scorn} costs him a second.[^1-15-2]
- Where each copy imposes a mandatory obligation, the obligations apply independently and
  may deadlock. Two copies of a card compelling a specific action leave the minion unable
  to act at all — see §3.9 for the stuck-and-cannot-act outcome.[^1-15-2]

#### 1.15.3 What stacks and what does not

Cumulation is not the same as arithmetic stacking. Read the wording:

- **Numeric modifiers stack additively.** A card granting +X to a trait grants it once per
  copy — e.g. {Mass Reality}; a vampire with three {Orun} has +6 capacity.[^1-15-3]
- **Grants written as a group threshold do not scale per copy.** Wording of the form
  "your Xs get N votes" or "N votes for every three" states the total, not a per-copy
  bonus: multiple {Legacy of Pander} give non-titled Panders 1 vote in total, and {Orun}
  gives 1 vote per three copies while its capacity bonus stacks.[^1-15-4] Non-stacking
  within one card's grant does not prevent additivity **across sources**: votes from a
  card ability and votes from a title are added (§5.8).[^1-15-4]
- **Effects keyed on the presence of a counter do not scale with the counter count.**
  "A minion with one or more X counters …" is a threshold, not a multiplier: extra
  counters may be placed and have no additional effect — e.g. {Dr. Morrow, The
  Skindoctor} (‑1 stealth, not ‑X), {Kahina the Sorceress} (one damage total). By the same
  reading, multiple copies of a card whose trigger is a single conditional burn fire once
  — {Orun} burns one counter, not one per copy.[^1-15-5]

#### 1.15.4 Cross-card non-stacking

Discipline grants from **different** cards do not combine to produce the superior level.
A superior discipline requires a single source that grants it; two inferior grants remain
two inferior grants — e.g. {Leech} and {Putrescent Servitude} together do not yield
superior [POT].[^1-15-6] This is a narrow rule about discipline level, not a general
"similar effects do not stack" principle; numeric bonuses from distinct cards stack
normally per §1.15.3.

#### 1.15.5 Per-action play limits

A limit expressed per action binds the **minion**, not the action: different minions
acting on the same action may each play their own copy — e.g. {Cloak the Gathering},
{Suppressing Fire}.[^1-15-7] The general scoping of such limits is §1.2; 1.15 records only
the consequence, that the resulting copies then cumulate under §1.15.1.
