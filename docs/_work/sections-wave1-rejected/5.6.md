### 5.6 Retainers

**Base rules.** A retainer is placed on the acting minion when an employ retainer action resolves, receives its printed life from the blood bank, cannot be transferred, and cannot act independently. It is not normally harmed in combat: an attacker may strike a retainer instead of its employer, but only at long range and only by announcing that target when the strike is announced, and a dodge does not protect it.

#### 5.6.1 Employer and retainer

A retainer is usable the moment it is in play. Nothing delays it — the "cannot act this turn" restriction belongs to the recruit ally action and is printed nowhere else, so no equivalent applies to an employed retainer.[^5-6-1] The retainer enters play only when the employ action successfully resolves, so it is unavailable in combat arising from that action being blocked; from resolution onward it functions normally for the remainder of that turn. §3.7.4 owns the employ action itself, including restrictions on who may attempt it.

A retainer is a card on a minion, not a minion. An action or effect that targets a retainer does not target the employing minion, so text keyed to actions targeting *the minion* — e.g. {Detect Authority} at [ani] — does not apply.[^5-6-2] The same holds for equipment; §1.12 owns cards-on-cards generally and §3.1 the targeting side.

#### 5.6.2 Retainers using equipment

The wording template is a retainer that may *use* a weapon or other equipment its employer bears (e.g. {Ghoul Retainer}). Two rules govern every instance of it.

**The retainer is not the bearer.** The employer remains the bearer, and every bearer-keyed clause on the equipment still reads against the employer: a burn-the-opponent clause conditioned on the bearer does not fire for the retainer's use, self-inflicted damage lands on the employer, and a cost or state requirement imposed on the bearer is simply not imposed on the retainer — e.g. {Ghoul Retainer} may use {Jar of Skin Eaters} with no counters on it.[^5-6-3]

**The use is not a strike.** It resolves the equipment's effect in full, including all side effects and non-damage effects — sends-to-torpor and combat-ends weapons work as written — but it is not a strike and does not enter the strike economy in either direction. It confers no maneuver, press, or additional strike on the employer, the employer's additional-strike effects grant the retainer nothing, and the retainer cannot play cards on the use itself (ammunition, strike modifiers), though it does benefit from ammunition the employer played on the weapon in an earlier round.[^5-6-4]

#### 5.6.3 Retainers dealing and taking damage

Damage inflicted by a retainer is **environmental damage with the retainer as its source**, not a strike. Prevention and immunity keyed to retainers, or to the retainer's type, apply against it; strike-keyed defences do not.[^5-6-5] See §4.4 for damage sources and §4.5 for prevention.

An effect a retainer has already generated is not undone by the retainer's burning, theft, or removal. Damage the retainer inflicts in a strike resolution is dealt even if that same resolution burns the retainer, and a duration effect sourced from a retainer runs to its stated end regardless of what becomes of the retainer meanwhile.[^5-6-6]
