# Section taxonomy for ruling classification

Codes and scopes. Pick the section whose PRINCIPLE the ruling teaches or illustrates.

## 1. Cards
- 1.1 — Card text vs. rules: card text overriding rulebook; "cannot" absolute; mandatory vs. optional effects; reading card text.
- 1.2 — Wording templates & periodicity: "each combat", "once each turn", "once per game", "this round", "since your last turn", "would/when/after", optional maneuvers/presses semantics.
- 1.3 — Card types & multi-type cards: locations that are equipment (locquipments), reflex cards, clan equipment, cards that are several types.
- 1.4 — Representation & placeholders: cards representing other things lose printed text; name lookups; no contest for placeholders.
- 1.5 — Abilities vs. cards: ability use is not a card play; abilities usable in torpor/while locked; ability reuse; abilities whose use requires the bearer to be *personally* a participant (eg. in combat themselves, not merely combat occurring); **a minion barred from ACTING may still USE its abilities, including lock-to-use ones (eg. the turn it is recruited)** — ability use is not an action. Cross-ref 5.2 for the lock, 3.7.4 for the recruit action itself.
- 1.6 — Requirements: requirements to play vs. to keep; abnormal entry (diablerie, effects putting cards in play) ignores requirements; capacity/discipline requirement changes.
- 1.7 — Costs & payment: when costs are paid; cost vs. "burn blood to" effects; X costs; cost arithmetic; pool/blood/life payment; cost reductions; effects naming an amount the target may not hold (steal/transfer templates take what is there).
- 1.8 — Playing & canceling a card: "as played" window, what cancellation does/reaches, canceled-still-played, costs of canceled cards, canceled strikes/masters. (ALREADY DRAFTED)
- 1.9 — Replacement: when cards are replaced; not replaced on blocked action; "do not replace until"; empty library; empty *crypt* as the same class of precondition for effects drawing from or putting into play from the crypt.
- 1.10 — Burn / remove from game / shuffle back: "when burned" triggers vs. removal; shuffle-into-library effects; crypt/library separation.
- 1.11 — Ash heap: retrieval (played vs. put there); owner vs. controller for retrieval.
- 1.12 — Cards on cards: host leaves play → hosted cards burn; counters on hosts; counter-count conventions on entry (a card entering with X=0 counters burns immediately) and burn thresholds keyed to another value (eg. vandal counters vs. a location's blood cost).
- 1.13 — Contests: unique cards/titles contested; self-contest; effects while contested; what's remembered after; vampire circles & same-name vampires.
- 1.14 — Set-aside & announced cards: cards announced from hand for later; temporarily out-of-play cards.
- 1.15 — Cumulative & stacking effects: whether multiple instances of an effect stack or cap; multiple copies of a card each exerting their effect vs. one combined result; per-copy costs and per-copy triggers; bonuses that stack additively vs. flat effects that do not scale with counter count; "does not stack with similar effects". Added 2026-07-20 on Phase 3 evidence — see below.

## 2. Timing
- 2.1 — Effect windows overview: impulse, who may act when (generic sequencing not fitting elsewhere).
- 2.2 — "As the action is announced" effects: must precede regular modifiers/reactions.
- 2.3 — "After resolution"/"after combat" modifiers: what may be played after an action resolves; ordering of post-resolution effects.
- 2.4 — Simultaneous effects & ordering: controller chooses order; unlock-phase ordering; cost-and-benefit simultaneity.
- 2.5 — Duration & persistence: "until end of X"; "for the rest of the game"; effects losing track of hidden cards; persistence through state changes.

## 3. Actions & Politics
- 3.1 — Announcement & targets: what is fixed at announcement; directed vs. undirected; action directed at a card = at its controller.
- 3.2 — Stealth & intercept: when stealth/intercept may be added; persistence during the action.
- 3.3 — Blocking: block declaration sequence; declining; blocking locks; who may block; block when target vanished.
- 3.4 — Resolution, success & effect: success vs. having an effect; "successful but no effect"; "successful resolution" wording.
- 3.5 — NRA & canceled actions: repetition limits, reached resolution, fail/end/cancel templates, canceled blocks/combats. (ALREADY DRAFTED)
- 3.6 — Continue as if unblocked: {Form of Mist}-style continuation; per-block effects provided again on second block; re-block opportunities.
- 3.7.1 — Bleed: bleed-specific rules (amount, target, reduction, {Deflection}-style redirection).
- 3.7.2 — Hunt: hunt success requires gain; hunt value bonuses; special hunt actions.
- 3.7.3 — Equip actions: equip from hand/minion; equipment movement actions.
- 3.7.4 — Employ retainer & recruit ally actions: the employ/recruit ACTIONS (recruitment specifics, cost, requirements); and **entry into play by a card effect rather than by the action** — action-keyed effects (cost reductions naming an action, stealth modifiers, cancel-a-played-card, the NRA) do NOT attach to non-action entry, while effects keyed to the recruiting minion do. Cross-ref 1.6 for requirements under abnormal entry. **Does NOT own "acting the turn recruited"** — a recruited minion's ability USE is 1.5 (with a 5.2 lock cross-ref), and whether a minion entering play may ACT that turn is 5.5/5.6. Scope line split 2026-07-20 — see below.
- 3.7.5 — Referendum procedure: announcement/terms/casting/tally; pool burned when; "during referendum" windows; automatic pass/fail; referendum part of action resolution.
- 3.7.6 — Votes & ballots: votes vs. ballots; Priscus; bonus vs. conditional votes; vote reduction/change; abstain; casting while locked.
- 3.7.7 — Leave torpor / rescue actions: the actions themselves (cost, who pays, reductions).
- 3.7.8 — Diablerie & blood hunt: diablerie action, blood hunt referendum, trophies for diablerie.
- 3.8 — Actions provided by cards & titles: multiple copies provide multiple actions; contested title = no action.
- 3.9 — Mandatory actions: what makes an action mandatory; discharging the obligation and being free to act normally afterwards; a different effect granting the same action type does NOT satisfy a distinct obligation; mandatory before non-mandatory in the minion phase; the "stuck and cannot act" outcome when the mandatory action is prevented or already impossible. Added 2026-07-20 on Phase 3 evidence — see below.
- 3.10 — Changing the acting minion: effects that substitute a different minion into an announced action ({Mask of a Thousand Faces}, {Deep Song}-style actor redefinition); which effects carry over (action modifiers) and which do not (inherent modifiers); eligibility keyed to what the substitute could independently have done. Added 2026-07-20 on Phase 3 evidence — see below.

## 4. Combat
- 4.1 — Combat sequence & rounds: the script; what resets between rounds; combat outside actions.
- 4.2 — Range: determine range; range-setting exclusivity; range for round vs. combat.
- 4.3 — Strikes: choosing strikes; strike resolution; first strike; additional strikes; hand vs. ranged.
- 4.4 — Damage: additional damage inherits properties; strength set vs. bonus; aggravated; environmental damage; retainer damage; damage outside combat.
- 4.5 — Prevention & immunity: prevention windows; "prevent up to X"; no carry-over; unpreventable; "immune" = never inflicted; wounded.
- 4.6 — Dodge: scope (whole strike incl. supplementary effects); what survives a dodge.
- 4.7 — Strike: Combat Ends: timing vs. damage; first strike interaction.
- 4.8 — Presses: current round only; press priority.
- 4.9 — End of round / combat / new combat: "when combat would end" vs. "at end of combat" vs. "after combat"; effects lost when new combat queued.
- 4.10 — Weapons & equipment in combat: once per combat/round; multiple copies; stolen/destroyed mid-combat; weapon damage value; "each combat" on equipment.

## 5. Minions
- 5.1 — Vampires: capacity & changes; uniqueness; merge (advanced); vampire creation; crypt card identity.
- 5.2 — Lock & unlock: wakes vs. unlock; preventing normal unlock; unlocking the unlocked; lock as cost vs. effect.
- 5.3 — Torpor (state): going to torpor; wounded; what's usable in torpor; effects on torpid vampires.
- 5.4 — Burning minions: who burned them; combat vs. non-combat burns; trophies/bounties on burns; burn vs. removal of minions.
- 5.5 — Allies: life vs. blood; "as a vampire"/"with X as a vampire"; ally limits; allies acting, **including whether an ally entering play may act that turn** (moved from 3.7.4, 2026-07-20).
- 5.6 — Retainers: employ/bearer; retainers striking or damaging; retainers surviving bearer; **whether a retainer entering play is usable that turn** (moved from 3.7.4, 2026-07-20).
- 5.7 — Special minion classes & traits: ghouls, animals, werewolves, Imbued, slaves, Black Hand, Red List, Scarce, True Brujah, Infernal, Sterile, Flight.
- 5.8 — Titles: acquiring/contesting/yielding; dormant titles; sect change effects on titles; title-granted capacity/votes/actions.
- 5.9 — Traits & trait-change precedence: sect, clan and other traits as things effects read and write; when two effects change the same trait at different times the LATER governs, and the earlier resurfaces if the later lapses; temporary override vs. permanent change; whether a changed trait counts as the trait for other cards' requirements and triggers. Sect is the most heavily ruled instance, not the topic. Added 2026-07-20 on Phase 3 evidence — see below. Cross-ref 2.4 (which owns ordering of *simultaneous* effects only) and 3.10 (acting-minion designation as a written trait).

## 6. Methuselahs & game
- 6.1 — Owner vs. controller: zones belong to owner; control defines actor/payer.
- 6.2 — Taking control of minions: temporary control; steals mid-action/combat; controller ousted.
- 6.3 — Taking control of cards in play: requirements don't apply; where equipment/locations go.
- 6.4 — Leaving & re-entering play: what cards remember; permanents not replaced.
- 6.5 — Pool, Edge & ousting: pool gain/loss/payment distinctions; the Edge; oust timing & consequences; victory points; withdrawal.
- 6.6 — Master phase: master phase actions; trifles; out-of-turn masters; masters on minions.
- 6.7 — Influence phase & crypt: transfers; influencing; uncontrolled region.

- 6.9 — Hand, draw & discard: hand size (what counts against it, bonuses, stacking,
  persistence); draw/discard mechanics (which card may be discarded, draw-then-discard

## Roles

Two of these decide whether a ruling reaches the document. One does not — see the
low-stakes note.

- P — the ruling STATES or directly applies a general principle of that section.
      These become the section's PROSE.
- E — a usable EXAMPLE of that principle on a specific card. These become the
      "e.g." cards the prose cites (style rule: 1-3 per principle, never a list).
- C — teaches nothing worth carrying into the document. Correctly stays in the
      rulings database. Requires a note. Two tests, either one is enough:
      * TRANSFER — does this ruling change how you would rule on a DIFFERENT card?
        If no, it is C however generally you can phrase it.
      * NON-OBVIOUSNESS — would a judge fluent in the rulebook already know it?
        If yes, it is a reminder, not a ruling.
- G — GAP: the ruling teaches something general, but no section here owns it.
      Requires a note naming the missing topic. **G MAY carry codes** — list the
      nearest partially-owning sections, meaning "homeless at its core, but these
      sections touch it". Use `-` only when nothing comes close.

**P vs E is low-stakes** — see the contract; when torn, choose E.

**C vs G is the distinction that matters**, and it is the one this schema previously
collapsed. "No section fits" has two completely different causes:
- C — nothing general here to capture. The corpus has a natural ceiling of these, and
  a healthy C fraction is correct by design.
- G — something general here that we failed to plan a home for. This is the ONLY
  evidence that justifies expanding the taxonomy.
Merging them makes the coverage number uninterpretable: a low score could mean either
"the corpus is more card-specific than we assumed" (do nothing) or "we are missing
sections" (expand). Keeping them apart is what lets the number answer the question.

## 6.9 — how it was added (the evidence standard for future additions)

Confirmed by BOTH independent calibration passes, which cited the same deciding rulings:
`Dreams of the Sphinx.3` ("cards that are not replaced count against the hand size") and
`Infernal Pursuit.1` ("the card you discard need not be one of the cards you just drew").
Both are statable as rules with no card named, and they sit in *different* sub-topics —
two distinct sub-topics is what turns a keyword cluster into a section.

The control worked: both passes independently sent `Elder Library.1` to 1.13 contests
despite its hand-size vocabulary. Shared vocabulary is not a shared gap.

**6.9 is one code covering three sub-topics on purpose.** The discard phase arguably
deserves its own section, and the drafter may well split it in the document. But the two
calibration passes disagreed on exactly that boundary (`Art of Love.2`), so a second code
would buy drift for no gain. Splitting later is cheap — everything lands in one small
extract, so a split reclassifies ~30 rulings rather than the corpus. Routing codes do not
have to match the table of contents.

## 1.15, 3.9, 3.10, 5.9 — how they were added (Phase 3 evidence)

Decided 2026-07-20 by Lionel on the Phase 3 G pile (91 rulings over 2,605). Same standard
as 6.9: a topic must be general, transfer across cards, and show more than one sub-topic.

- **1.15 cumulative & stacking** — 28 G rulings, independently rediscovered in 13 of 18
  chunks. Sub-topics on both polarities: effects that DO stack (`Betrayer.3` per-copy
  damage, `Ossian.1`-style additive bonuses) and effects that do NOT (`Anarch Convert`
  vote caps, flat counter effects). 23 of the 28 were forced into 1.2, which owns
  periodicity templates and never claimed stacking.
- **5.9 traits & trait-change precedence** — 25 sect-labelled G rulings PLUS 3 that the
  agents filed as generic precedence: `Derange.7` (clan), `Deep Song.2` and
  `Powerbase: Savannah.1` (acting minion). The same "later effect governs" rule on three
  different traits. **The agents named the instance, not the rule**: they converged on
  "sect as a trait" because sect is where the rulings are, but a sect-only section would
  have led a drafter to state a sect-specific rule that is in fact general. Scoped to
  traits generally, with sect as the worked example.
- **3.9 mandatory actions** — 7 rulings over 5 cards. This ABSORBS the failed
  "minion-phase action ordering" watch item: `Lunatic Eruption.1` and
  `Spirit Marionette.2` are not about phase ordering generally, they are about mandatory
  actions specifically. Distinct sub-topics: discharge (`Cry Wolf.2`), non-satisfaction
  by a different effect (`Elen Kamjian.2`), and the stuck-and-cannot-act outcome
  (`Change of Target.8`, `Elen Kamjian.3`).
- **3.10 changing the acting minion** — 4 rulings, thin on count but dense on content:
  `Mask of a Thousand Faces.3` alone carries five ruling references and the
  action-modifiers-carry / inherent-modifiers-do-not rule. 3.1 fixes what is set at
  announcement; nothing owned changing the actor afterwards.

**Rejected from the same pile** (recorded so they are not re-proposed): the 5
question-card rulings are all `Monocle of Clarity` and the 2 auction rulings are all
`Extremis Boon` — single-card topics, which the style rules leave in the rulings database.
Both were mislabelled G. **Lesson for future classification: G requires general AND
homeless; homeless alone is C.** Nine of the 91 G rows are single-card.
