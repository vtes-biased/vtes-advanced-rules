## Drafting constraints

- Judge-level terse prose, mechanics-first. Full fluency with the base rules is assumed;
  precision over accessibility.
- **NO exhaustive card lists.** Tie the rule to its wording template, then "e.g." + 1-3 cards.
- Be synthetic: cross-reference other sections rather than repeating; merge redundant points.
- Card names in braces `{Abbot}`; disciplines and card types in brackets `[pot]`, `[POT]`,
  `[ACTION]`.
- Every derived statement carries a footnote in a `#### References` block at the section end,
  listing the ruling reference IDs plus the card or group the ruling is recorded under, e.g.
  `[^x]: [LSJ 20061207] [RBK playing-a-card] — group "Cancel" (G00058).`
- Pure one-card text interpretations do **not** enter the document; they stay in the
  rulings database.
- `⚠ REVIEW` marks a point that still needs judge confirmation.

### [medium] other

R2369 ({Nergal}.4: 'If a card he plays is cancelled as it played and no cost is paid, his ability is not considered used for the turn') and R2478 ({Vidal Jarbeaux}.3: 'If a card he plays is canceled as it is played, he is still considered having used the requirement, if any') answer the same question — does a canceled play consume the enabling resource? — with opposite-looking answers. Neither carries a !TENSION tag, because neither classifier could see the other. A drafter working from this extract will write one sentence and be contradicted by the other ruling.

**Recommendation:** Co-locate these two for adjudication before drafting. The reconciling rule is probably 'a requirement is spent on announcement, a per-turn ability is spent on payment', but that distinction is not stated anywhere in the membership and must be confirmed rather than inferred.

*Rulings:* R2369, R2478

### [low] other

A drafting trap rather than a classification defect. R0338 ({Charming Lobby}) says a political action played from hand via that card 'cannot be canceled as it is played', while R0572 ({Echo of Harmonies}) says the retained political action 'can be canceled as it is played'. R0337 supplies the reconciling mechanism — under Charming Lobby the card is considered played only at successful resolution, so no as-played window ever opens — but the three sit far apart in the extract and the connection is easy to miss.

**Recommendation:** Keep all three; they are correctly filed. Note in the extract that R0337 is the explanation for R0338, so the drafter states the timing rule ('when the card counts as played determines whether an as-played window exists') rather than two card-specific exceptions.

*Rulings:* R0337, R0338, R0572

### `no-effect-plays` — 46 rulings, 1 in this section

**⚑ RIDE-ALONG — this slug is THREE questions, not one (Phase 4.5 Q5).** Its size
is an artifact of merging them. Do not meet one polarity and state an over-broad rule.
(a) and (b) do not contradict: they are two sides of one rule whose deciding distinction is
**a requirement to play** versus **mere futility** — that boundary is **1.6's** to own, even
though 20 of the 46 were filed 1.1 first. Sections that must agree before any of this is
drafted: 1.6 (the deciding section), 3.4 (owns (c)), 1.1 (currently over-claiming this pile),
4.5, 4.6, 4.3, 3.2, 1.8. **1.8 is already drafted** and says nothing about no-effect plays;
it is the natural owner of the permissive default, so adopting (a) there is a light edit to
reviewed text — **the owner's call, not the drafter's.**

**(a) PERMISSIVE DEFAULT — a card may be played, or an action taken, knowing it will accomplish nothing.**

- `R0533` **Draba.3** — playable although it will do nothing; the prohibition cases (eg. Dead Hand.2, this chunk) point the other way.
- `R2087` **Night Terrors.3** — playable on a no/negative-stealth minion though it will do nothing; prohibition-style rulings elsewhere point the other way.
- `R1459` **Repulsion.1** — playable even though the stealth isn't needed
- `R0269` **Call the Lamprey.1** — permissive side: playable against a target with none of the relevant resource, mirroring Draba.3
- `R0018` **Absorb the Mind.1** — playable against a target for which it will have zero effect (no blood to affect)
- `R0936` **Instantaneous Transformation.1** — playable if unblocked even though it will have no effect for lack of a suitable target — same question as Draba.3/Blood Fury.6
- `R0986` **Kindred Segregation.2** — callable even with no allies in play to affect — same question as Draba.3/Blood Fury.6
- `R1637` **Siphon.1** — a zero-blood vampire remains a valid target even though the action may accomplish nothing
- `R1296` **Peace Treaty.2** — a referendum can be called even with no eligible subject in play, mirroring the Draba/Blood Fury tension over playing cards for zero effect
- `R1368` **Projectile.3** — dodge permitted despite having no effect against this weapon; reinforces the permission side (cf. Draba.3) against the prohibition-case rulings
- `R1545` **Scorpion Sting.2** — dodge is permitted although it accomplishes nothing against this card
- `R1612` **Shadow Feint.1** — dodge is not prevented, it just accomplishes nothing — same question as Draba.3, opposite mechanism
- `R1971` **Vengeance of Samiel.2** — dodge remains playable even though it accomplishes nothing against this effect
- `R1805` **Target Head.5** — playable on a strike doing no damage (dodge/combat ends) though it will have no effect there
- `R1814` **Target Vitals.4** — identical wording to Target Head.5
- `R0465` **Deep Cover Agent.2** — clarifies the burn-option condition is exactly as printed (no ready Seraph), independent of whether a valid target exists — an ability-activation instance of the no-effect-plays question, same polarity as Draba.3 (permitted despite no effect).
- `R0714` **Forced March.2** — permission side: usable/unlocks despite the action having no effect from a missing suitable target (cf. Draba.3).

**(b) NAMED-OBJECT PROHIBITION — a card whose text names an object it acts on cannot be played when no such object exists. Includes the eight-copy prevention template, into which the standalone `prevention-without-damage` slug (R0065, R0082) merges: ten rulings, one template, ONE answer.**

- `R0170` **Blessing of Durga Syn.1** — a prohibition bars play when there is no equipment to steal — prohibition side of the question (contrast Draba.3's permissive side)
- `R0195` **Blood Fury.6** — a prohibition bars playing even for zero effect — the inverse of the Draba case
- `R0205` **Blood Rage.4** — a prohibition bars playing even for zero effect — the inverse of the Draba case (identical wording to Blood Fury.6)
- `R0224` **Bollix.2** — a prohibition bars playing even for zero effect — the inverse of the Draba case (identical wording to Blood Fury.6)
- `R0280` **Canine Horde.1** — prohibition side: cannot be played absent equipment to destroy
- `R0730` **Fractured Armament.1** — a card requiring an object for its effect is flatly unplayable without one; pulls against cases elsewhere where a card may be played knowing it will have no effect
- `R1623` **Shattering Blow.1** — destroy-equipment card requires equipment to exist; prohibition-side parallel to the prevention-card template below
- `R0458` **Dead Hand.2** — a prohibition bars playing prevention cards even for zero net effect (cycling) — same slug as Blood Fury.6/Draba.3 in calibration.md.
- `R1487` **Rigor Mortis.4** — a prohibition on additional strikes bars playing one even for a side benefit
- `R0737` **Free States Rant.1** — prohibition side: card cannot be played if targets are insufficient to allocate all its points
- `R1815` **Taste of Vitae.1** — flatly barred from targeting allies, the prohibitive counterpart to the Draba-style permissive cases
- `R0251` **Brujah Frenzy.1** — prohibition side: cannot be played absent a suitable minion to act on
- `R0254` **Brujah Frenzy.4** — prohibition side: cannot be played absent a ready, unlocked minion; also touches 5.2's lock-state requirement
- `R0233` **Bonding.1** — prohibition bars play when the effect (stealth) would not be needed — prohibition side of the question
- `R0774` **Glancing Blow.1** — prohibition side, prevention-card flavor: cannot be used if there is no damage to prevent
- `R0833` **Heaven's Gate.1** — duplicate of the Glancing Blow/Hidden Strength prevention-card template
- `R0856` **Hidden Strength.3** — third copy of the prevention-card no-damage-to-prevent prohibition template
- `R1634` **Sideslip.1** — prevention card requires unresolved damage to be playable at all
- `R1646` **Skin of Night.1** — same prevention-requires-damage template as R1634
- `R1647` **Skin of Rock.1** — same template
- `R1671` **Soak.1** — same template
- `R1736` **Stonestrength.2** — same prevention-requires-damage template
- `R0065` **Apparition.1** — playable at any time in combat even with no damage yet to prevent — the permissive case
- `R0082` **Armor of Vitality.2** — cannot be used with no damage to prevent — the prohibition case, opposite of R0065

**(c) SUCCESS WITHOUT EFFECT — whether a no-effect action still resolves successfully and still locks the actor. A different question entirely; it is 3.4's own scope line, not a tension.**

- `R0228` **Bomb.3** — permissive side: the action proceeds and can even succeed while having no effect, mirroring Draba.3
- `R0278` **Camera Phone.1** — permissive side, identical wording to Bomb.3: action proceeds and can succeed while having no effect
- `R0735` **Freak Drive.2** — permissive side: playable even knowing it will have no effect for lack of a target
- `R1715` **Spying Mission.3** — mandatory bonus cannot apply to an already-unsuccessful/zero bleed — same shape as the prevention-card template
- `R1023` **Legacy of Caine.1** — mandatory action forced despite predictable zero effect
- ★ `R1958` **Veil of Darkness.1** — here a no-effect action does NOT lock the acting vampire, unlike the general rule that any attempted action locks regardless of effect
