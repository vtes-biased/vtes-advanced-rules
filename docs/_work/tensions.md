## RESOLVED — mandatory-action-satisfaction (3.9)

**Not coined by the classifiers.** Surfaced by the Phase 4.5 reviewer of section 3.9 as a
flat contradiction between two rulings, and it would have reached the drafter unmarked.

- `R1704` {Spirit Marionette}.3 — "[OBE] The mandatory bleed can be performed by playing a
  card (eg. {Computer Hacking})." [LSJ 20021121-2]
- `R2525` {Elen Kamjian}.2 — "Another bleed action (eg. {Flurry of Action}) does not count
  as the bleed action her ability forces her to take." [ANK 20211009-2]

**ADJUDICATED 2026-07-20 by Lionel.** Not a contradiction. The discriminator is on the
MANDATING effect, not on the card used to satisfy it:

> When an effect makes an action mandatory, whether another action of that type discharges
> the obligation depends on whether the mandating effect **provides** the action or merely
> **requires that one of that type be taken**.
> - PROVIDES ({Elen Kamjian}) — the obligation is to that specific provided action. Another
>   action of the same type, however obtained, does not discharge it.
> - REQUIRES A TYPE ({Spirit Marionette}) — any action of that type discharges it, including
>   one made by playing a card.

{Computer Hacking} vs {Flurry of Action} is NOT the variable — the first analysis to reach
this got that backwards. The satisfying card is irrelevant; the mandating card decides.

Predicts a third case in the same section: `R1926` {Undying Thirst}.1 ("if the vampire is
empty, he must either hunt or diablerize") requires a type and provides nothing, so any
hunt should discharge it. Drafter: state the principle, cite {Elen Kamjian} and
{Spirit Marionette} as the contrasting pair. Do NOT mark ⚠ REVIEW — this is adjudicated.
